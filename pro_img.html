TEACHING

During my time thus far in University, I have been fortunate enough to get to work with not only great students, and faculty but help leave a positive influence on those around us. A passion I discovered recently is teaching. Rarely do I not find an opportunity to be able to help others but leave an impact and change a student's perspective on programming.

I worked as a Peer-Teacher for the College of Engineering in the Freshmen Python Course ENGR 102. While in this position I and a teaching team of 6 were able to introduce many students' first-ever programming language. This was a unique opportunity as many students were unsure of what path in engineering they wanted to pursue. Having the chance to show the students the wonders of programming, its practicality, and use in our everyday lives was truly rewarding.

TAMU SHPE

Following my passion for teaching and empowering others I ran and was elected as Director of Technical Affairs for the Society of Hispanic Professional Engineers (SHPE). My mission is to create the technical leaders of tomorrow today by helping members develop programming skills to become successful in their academics and industry. I was able to host the organizations' first-ever web development workshop. We discussed responsive web-design, content layout, and professional presence. This allowed the members to design a personal portfolio but also help give recruiters a good first impression while learning front-end skills along the way. This past April I hosted a Python workshop focusing on the basics such as loops, if/else, and functions. The workshop was condcuted via Zoom through our organization's High School outreach program to pique their interests in STEM careers.
